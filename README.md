# DCR Small Group - Weekly Schedule

**Designing Clinical Research · Weekly Objectives, Activities & Deadlines**

This repository provides tools and resources for students participating in the *Designing Clinical Research* course. This weekly guide summarizes small group objectives, activities, and submission expectations from Week 1 to Week 8.

---

## Week 1: Framing Your Research Question

**Objective:** Identify a viable research question and establish its significance through a literature review.

### Small Group Activity
- Share your 1-sentence study summary with the group  
  *(e.g., “In adult patients with type 2 diabetes, does a structured walking program reduce HbA1c compared to usual care?”)*
- Share your **½ - ¾ page Significance section**; peers give feedback on clarity, relevance, and gap in knowledge.
- Discuss whether the research question is **FINE**: *Feasible, Interesting, Novel, Ethical*.
- Tip: Use PubMed to find **3 - 5 key papers** supporting why your question matters, and cite them in your Significance section.

### AI Assistant
- Use AI to find a few relevant papers for your initial research question. [link]
- Use AI to develop a search strategy for a systematic literature search. [link]

**Due:** Bring a draft to small group on Tuesday. Submit revised version electronically by Wednesday at midnight.

## Week 2: Study Design & Subject Selection

**Objective:** Draft a 1-page protocol outline and identify your study population, sample, and sampling strategy.

### Small Group Activity
- Share your **1-page protocol outline** (Research Question, Significance, Design, Subjects, Variables).
- Share your **Research Design** (e.g., cross-sectional) and **Study Subjects** section; peers review inclusion/exclusion criteria and sampling strategy.
- Example:  
  *Inclusion: adults 18 - 65 with diagnosed hypertension. Exclusion: pregnant women, patients on dialysis.*  
  Be ready to justify tricky exclusions.
- Discuss which study design is most appropriate and feasible.
- Discuss target population, accessible population, and sample:  
  *(e.g., Target = all US adults with hypertension; Accessible = patients at UCSF outpatient clinic; Sample = 250 consenting adults with hypertension).*  
- Discuss which sampling strategy is most appropriate and feasible (simple random, systematic, cluster random, or nonprobability sampling), and explain why.
- Tip: Consider improving representativeness by recruiting from multiple sites.

**Due:** Bring a draft to small group on Tuesday. Submit revised version electronically by Wednesday at midnight.

## Week 3: Measurements & Sample Size Mechanics

**Objective:** Define key predictor/outcome variables and practice sample size calculation mechanics.

### Small Group Activity
- Share your **½ - ¾ page Measurements section**; Group members will review your primary predictor, primary outcome, and key covariates,
provide feedback on whether they are clearly defined and appropriate for the study aims, and suggest improvements.
- Share your completed **Sample Size Table**  
  - Template 1 for dichotomous outcomes  
  - Template 2 for continuous outcomes
- Use [https://sample-size.net/](https://sample-size.net/) with **alpha = 0.05 (two-tailed)** and **power = 80%**:
  - **Practice 1 (Binary Outcome):**
    - Group 0 (no exercise/usual care): expected BP control = 30%
    - Group 1 (walking program): expected BP control = 50%
    - Assume two-sided α = 0.05, 80% power, equal group sizes
    - Calculate sample size per group and total sample size
  - **Practice 2 (Continuous Outcome):**
    - Group 0: mean systolic BP = 140 mmHg
    - Group 1: mean systolic BP = 135 mmHg
    - SD = 15 mmHg
    - Assume two-sided α = 0.05, 80% power, equal group sizes
    - Calculate sample size per group and total sample size
- Discuss: Now use [https://sample-size.net/](https://sample-size.net/) to explore the assumptions and inputs needed to
  calculate the sample size for your study. Based on your initial estimate, is the required sample size feasible?
  Why or why not? Next week, you will refine and justify your sample size calculation and finalize your target sample size.

**Due:** Bring a draft to small group on Tuesday. Submit revised version electronically by Wednesday at midnight.

## Week 4: Justifying Your Sample Size & Alternate Design

**Objective:** Write a strong sample size justification and sketch an alternative study design.

### Small Group Activity
- Share your **½-page Sample Size Justification**, including:
  - Null hypothesis
  - Statistical test
  - Alpha/beta
  - Assumptions
  - Feasibility statement
- Example null hypothesis:  
  *“There is no association between participation in a structured walking program and change in systolic blood pressure at 6 months.”*
- Discuss: Did you find published data to back your assumptions? Did you talk to a statistician or your mentor? What's the minimum effect size worth detecting
- Tip: A sensitivity analysis (showing N under slightly different assumptions) can strengthen your justification
- Share your **1-page alternate study outline** using a different design (e.g., if main study is cohort, outline case-control or RCT).
- Discuss whether assumptions are supported by published data, whether you consulted a statistician/mentor, and what minimum meaningful effect size is worth detecting.

**Due:** Bring a draft to small group on Tuesday. Submit revised version electronically by Wednesday at midnight.

## Week 5: Enhancing Causal Inference

**Objective:** Address threats to causal inference (confounding, randomization, or diagnostic validity depending on study type).

### Small Group Activity
- Share your **<1-page Enhancing Inference section** (format depends on study type).
- **Observational study example:**
  - List important confounders. Exmaple of an imporant one: Age (main exposure: physical activity level; main outcome: blood pressure at 6 months;
    age is associated with both the exposure and outcome and is not caused by the exposure).
  - Describe 2+ strategies (multivariable regression adjustment, propensity score matching, restriction)
- **RCT example:**
  - Specify randomization method (e.g., sealed envelopes or REDCap)
  - Blinding approach (e.g., double-blind)
  - Use of run-in design or stratification by site/severity
- **Diagnostic study example:**
  - Discuss gold standard (e.g., cardiac catheterization vs. CT angiography)
  - Evaluate whether it is truly gold standard
  - Explain assessor blinding to index test results
- Discuss whether your study supports causal inference or only association, and how transparently limitations are presented.

**Due:** Bring a draft to small group on Tuesday. Submit revised version electronically by Wednesday at midnight.

## Week 6: Data Collection & Management

**Objective:** Build a clear data collection form, data dictionary, and data management plan.

### Small Group Activity
- Share **1 page of your data collection form**, focusing on visual clarity and wording quality.
- Share your **data dictionary** for up to 5 variables:
  - Variable name
  - Label
  - Type
  - Allowed values
  - Coding (e.g., 0 = No, 1 = Yes)
- Example variable:  
  *Name = "bp_systolic", Label = "Systolic blood pressure at visit 1", Type = integer, Allowed = 60 - 250, Units = mmHg*
- Share your **½ - ¾ page data management plan**, addressing:
  - Paper vs electronic forms
  - REDCap vs Qualtrics
  - Secure server storage
  - De-identification strategy
- Discuss data entry roles, error checking processes, and handling participant withdrawal.

**Due:** Bring a draft to small group on Tuesday. Submit revised version electronically by Wednesday at midnight.

## Week 7: Complete Protocol Draft & Pretesting Plan

**Objective:** Assemble a complete 5-page protocol draft and identify one unsettled issue for group discussion.

### Small Group Activity
- Share your **5-page protocol draft**, including:
  - Abstract
  - Specific Aims
  - Significance
  - Methods (Design, Subjects, Measurements, Sample Size, Inference, Data Management)
- Share your **pretesting plan (<1 page):**
  - Who are pretest subjects?
  - How many?
  - What will be tested? *(e.g., survey timing, pilot recruitment script with 3 colleagues)*
- Share your **unsettled issue (<1 page):** one open question for peer feedback.
  - Example: *“My sample size feels too large; should I narrow my population?”*
  - Example: *“I changed my research question; does my design still fit?”*
- This is the final chance for written feedback before final submission—come prepared with focused questions.
- Tip: Edit ruthlessly. Combined prior homework sections may exceed 5 pages; prioritize essentials.

**Due:** Bring a copy to small group on Tuesday. No electronic submission this week; leaders will not provide written feedback.

## Week 8: Final 5-Page Protocol Submission

**Objective:** Submit a polished, complete research protocol suitable as a foundation for a grant application.

### Small Group Activity
- Final document must include:
  - Title
  - Investigator team
  - AI disclosure
  - Abstract (~150 - 200 words)
  - Specific Aims
  - Significance (≤½ page)
  - Methods
- Methods must cover:
  - Overview of design
  - Study subjects (target/accessible populations, inclusion/exclusion, sampling, recruitment, retention)
  - Measurements
  - Statistical issues (sample size, inference, analytic approach)
  - Data management
- Optional but recommended:
  - Ethical considerations (especially for prospective studies)
  - Budget note
- References and clearly labeled appendices (e.g., data collection form, questionnaire) may be included beyond 5 pages; keep concise.
- Formatting requirements:
  - Standard 8.5" × 11" pages
  - 1-inch margins
  - 12-point font
  - Maximum 5 pages (excluding references/appendices)

**Due:** Submit electronically by the course deadline. 5 pages maximum (not including references and appendices).
