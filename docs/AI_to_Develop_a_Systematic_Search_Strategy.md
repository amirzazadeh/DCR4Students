**Use AI to develop a search strategy for a systematic literature search**

**AI Prompt Instructions**: Replace ***"TEXT"*** with your own research question, then copy and paste the prompt into an AI tool.

**Tips**:
- Protect your ideas and data: Use a secure, institution-approved AI platform whenever possible (e.g., UCSF ChatGPT Enterprise). Do not enter confidential, proprietary, or identifiable information into public AI tools.
- Verify the search terms: AI may suggest incorrect or outdated terms. Check redundancy, unjustified exclusion of terms. Check whether AI used “NOT.” In most cases, it should not be used.
- Review the final search carefully: Make sure the search strategy matches your question and does not miss important synonyms or concepts.
- Comprehensive search: In a comprehensive search for biomedical literature, you need to search both PubMed and EMBASE. Searching Web of Science is also recommended:
  - Search PubMed: [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/)
  - Search Embase: [https://www.embase.com/search/quick](https://www.embase.com/search/quick)
  -	Search Web of Science: [https://www.webofscience.com/wos/woscc/smart-search](https://www.webofscience.com/wos/woscc/smart-search)
-	Copy and paste the verified search terms into the search box of the databases/websites above (e.g., PubMed, Cochrane Library, Embase) to retrieve the most relevant evidence.
- After running the search:
  - PubMed: Click Advanced to review the search history and confirm the search was interpreted correctly.
  - Embase: Click Search History (or Advanced Search) to review how the search terms were mapped and combined.
  - Web of Science: Click Search History to review and verify the search query and results.

**AI Prompt Start**

- SYSTEM / CONTEXT: You are asked to delveop an effective search strategy that can be used to Seach evidence in PubMed and Embase and Web of Science.
- Develop a search strategy on ***"the impact of physical exercise on diabetes control among adults with diabetes"*** using the following PICO format: 
  - Population: ***"adults with type 2 diabetes"***
  - Intervention: ***"regular moderate aerobic exercise"***
  - Comparison: ***"usual care"***
  - Outcome: ***"HbA1c levels"***
- For PubMed, use truncation to avoid redundancy, and only provide valid MeSH terms from this address, and not their entry terms: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/mesh/
- For Embase, use find standard Emtree terms from https://www.embase.com/emtree?phase=continueToApp. Use ' ' quotation, /exp for standard terms in Emtree, AND, OR, as appropriate.  
- For Web of Science Search: Use AND, OR, " " quotation for an exact phrase search, * used as a wild card, as appropriate. No need to put TS.
- Give search strategy for PubMed, Embase, and the Web of Science. 

**AI Prompt End**
